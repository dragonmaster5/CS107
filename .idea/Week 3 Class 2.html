<hr> Bytes and bits

Bits: these are the smallest unit of information usually thought of as 0,1 in binary.
They are stored on a chip as electrical charge. 0 being negative and 1 being positive.
Byte: There are 8 bits in one of these, it is the smallest unit of storage in a computer. one packet of information that is smaller than 8 bits will be stored in a single byte, that overflows is stored in a another byte.
ASCII: American Standard for Information Interchange: Only a 128 characters, which are represented by 7 bits. Since 7 bits has 2^7 combinations. given the left over bit there are 128 characters still on the table.
